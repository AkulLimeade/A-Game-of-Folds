The T-rex was thought to be one of the most dangerous dinosaurs of its time. It had very short arms, and apparently wasn't very intelligent, but what it lacked in brains and arms, it made up for in size, and fury. Standing at 12-20 feet, the T-rex towered over many other dinosaurs, and was unmatched as the apex predator aka, top of the food chain. Learn how to fold this prehistoric beast of a lizard down below.
Difficulty level: 5/10

The pterodactyl was a cold blooded killer of the skies. The prehistoric equivalent of an eagle, this aerial menace was feared by most animals, as it flew in flocks across the skies of the prehistoric world. It had a wingspan of 36 feet and a beak size of 1 meter, making it the largest known flying animal at the time, and king of the skies. Learn how to fold this ferocious sky lizard down below.
Difficulty:6/10

The brachiosaurus was the largest known herbivore of the time, standing at 40-50 feet tall, with a full body length of 85 feet, this dino would've been the stuff of nightmares had it been a trained carnivore. Alas, it was a herbivore, and could do no more than use its long neck to reach the highest branches and leaves much like a giraffe. Learn how to fold this giant herbivore down below
Difficulty:5/10

The stegosaurus was a herbivorous dinosaur, but don't let that fool you. This 7,000 pound reptile has rows of armored plates across its back as protection against the biggest of predators. It had a spiked tail as well to provide extra protection against any other threats. Relatively small by dinosaur standards, the Stegosaurus was 9 ft tall, but 40 ft long. Learn how to fold this armored lizard down below
Difficulty:6.5/10

The Velociraptor was considered to be the smartest dinosaur of its era. Often hunting in packs rather than alone, this hyperintelligent creature was nimble, quick, and deadly against the right opponent. The modern equivalent of a wolf, you wouldn't want to get into an encounter with this lizard and its 10 inch claws.
Difficulty: 2.5/5
